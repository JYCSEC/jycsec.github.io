I've been a runner since middle school and have always enjoyed it as my sport of choice. Throughout middle school and high school, I ran track and field as a sprinter. My main events were the 100m, 200m, and 400m in the spring. For winter track, I ran the 55m, 200m, and the 300m. While the training was usually brutal, I usually found the track meets to be very rewarding.

Now, I don't run track and field and its been years since I've done a real sprint. However, I've picked up distance running for not just health reasons, but for therapeutic purposes. When I run, I can disconnect myself and take a step back from my day-to-day to just do something monotonous and let my mind wander. While this was very different from track and field, I found this to better suit me currently.

I've taken a break from consistent running and haven't been able to pick it back up in full force since I took my first summer-long break in 2017. Since then, it's been on-and-off with me doing 1-3 runs per week but often taking several weeks off before picking it back up. I believe by setting goals for myself, I will finally be able to reintegrate it into my life and this time, not let it go. I'll be posting my running goals and progress [here](2019-running-goals.md)!
