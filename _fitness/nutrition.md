I'm sure everyone's heard the age-old phrase, "Abs are made in the kitchen". Personally, this is something I agree with and over 4 years of manipulating my diet and my nutrition intake, I've been able to try a bunch of different diets and find the one that worked for me.

Now, to start, a disclaimer. My diet growing up was a little odd. Up until 6th grade in middle school, I had a fairly normal diet but made a change to a vegan diet in 7th grade (2010). I've kept at it until shortly after my freshman year of college where I decided to change my diet back to a traditional one (2016). Since I was vegan for 7 years already, I decided to not do it again as I had my own problems with the diet that I may comment on in a different post.

So, the different diets I've tried were primarily tied to manipulating my weight and are common in the weightlifting community.

Macronutrients are in Carb/Fat/Protein.

| Diet          | Year          | Weight Loss  | Cal   | Macros      | Strategy             |
| :-----------: | :-----------: | :----------: | :---: | :---------: | :------------------: |
| Clean Eating  | 1/17-4/17     | 145->130     | 1650  | 185/30/160  | None                 |
| Low-Fat Diet  | 5/17-8/17     | 135->135     | 2200  | 315/25/180  | High Snacking        |
| Keto Diet     | 1/18-5/18     | 165->155     | 2000  | 100/110/150 | IIFYM                |
| Low-Carb Diet | 8/18-10/18    | 175->165     | 1950  | 80/110/160  | Intermittent Fasting |
| Low-Carb Diet | 6/18-Present  | 165->?       | 1950  | 105/100/160 | IIFYM + IF           |

Clean eating had a lot of the normal "bodybuilder" foods. Most of my meals were the classic chicken, broccoli, and brown rice. Occasionally, I'd have salmon, tuna, and other meats but overall, the diet was restricting and made it difficult to eat out with friends.

I switched to a low-fat diet afterwards, but eating out with friends was still a challenge. It was still easier to manage versus clean eating still due to being able to have more fruits and occasionally sneaking on some candies.

For Keto, it was significantly easier to find places to eat. My main concern was that since the food was always very calorie-dense, I was often hungry at the end of the day and my cravings for fruit made it difficult to keep this diet.

For both low-carb diets, intermittent fasting made it significantly easier to manage the hunger. In the first try, my carb restrictions made it a little difficult to be able to eat fruit, but changing that and following IIFYM along with intermittent fasting made it significantly easier.


I hope to continue using a low-carb diet until I get down to about 145lbs. I think I've gained a decent amount of muscle since then and may be as lean as I was in 2017 when I was 130lbs. Still only time can tell as I continue my diet!
