For the remainder of 2019, my goals are to reintegrate running into my day-to-day. You can read more about the details [here](why-run.md).
My strategy is to first, regain distance [conservatively](https://www.jospt.org/doi/full/10.2519/jospt.2014.5164) and disregard the pace. After this, I can start to incorporate more strenuous runs and up the pace. Each stage is a goal and should last about a month but depending on fitness, they may be shortened or extended.

| Stages | Days/wk | Time             | Pace       | Distance  |
| :----: | :-----: | :--------------: | :--------: | :-------: |
| 1      | 3       | 15minx2, 30minx1 | | |
| 2      | 5       | 15minx3, 40minx2 | | |
| 3      | 5       | 20minx2, 40minx3 | 7:30, 8:00 | 20 mi     |
| 4      | 5       | 20minx2, 40minx3 | 7:00, 7:30 | 22 mi     |

One issue I have moving forward with this is the [research](http://indorgs.virginia.edu/MuscleClub/OKeefe_JH_article1+2.pdf) showing declining health benefits at a significant mileage that only gets exasperated by pace.

Still, for now, my goal is to set my weekly mileage goals to be in that 20-25mi range.
