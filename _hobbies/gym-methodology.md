Over the years, I've tried different ways of incorporating working out into my daily life. Balancing all my responsibilities and hobbies has always been a struggle, and I've tried many different strategies throughout the years. There are varying [research](http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/download?doi=10.1.1.473.3392&rep=rep1&type=pdf) claiming it takes about 72 hours to recover, and I've chosen my workout splits based on that research (sorry Bro split). Splits I've tried were push-pull-legs, upper-lower, and full body. Later on, I incorporated power & hypertrophy splits to these each of these to ensure a balance of volume and strength training. Each had their pros and cons from a time-balance perspective.

| Split          | Time      | Days     | Exercises/Split |
| :------------: | :-------: | :------: | :-----:         |
| Push-Pull-Legs | 60-75 min | 5-6 days | 7-8             |
| Upper-Lower    | 90+ min   | 4 days   | 10 or 6         |
| Full Body      | 120+ min  | 2-3 days | 14              |

Based on this, full body splits seem to take the least amount of time over a week at an estimate 4-6 hours. Upper-lower splits take a bit more at about 6 hours, and push-pull-legs take the most at about 6-7.5 hours. Another factor to account for is the commute time to the gym. Currently, my commute to the gym is about 20 minutes and it takes me a bit over 15 minutes to get ready to start lifting. This increases the overall totals to be:

| Split          | Time/wk       |
| :------------: | :-----------: |
| Push-Pull-Legs | 13-14.5 hours |
| Upper-Lower    | 10.5 hours    |
| Full Body      | 6.5-9.5 hours |

While this means that the full body split is the most time efficient split time-wise for me, it isn't the actual workout split I chose. Currently, I am sticking with the least time efficient, push-pull-legs split. The primary reason for this is the hidden factor that is behind the number of exercises per split... Its really, really, really tiring. Generally. I can perform at ~95% power output for maybe 2-3 workouts. All workouts after that, I'm usually too tired to perform above let's say ~70% of my max output. Now, once this gets to 6+ workouts, I usually can barely keep myself moving at maybe ~35% of my total output. Now, these numbers are arbitrary but if you fill this into the charts:

| Split          | Time/wk    | Exercises | Output | Output/Time |
| :------------: | :--------: | :-------: | :----: | :---: |
| Push-Pull-Legs | 14.5 hours | 8         | 33.90  | 2.38  |
| Upper-Lower    | 10.5 hours | 10        | 25.40  | 2.42  |
| Full Body      | 9.5 hours  | 14        | 23.25  | 2.45  |

While this does mean that push-pull-legs is still the least efficient workout time-wise for me, the gap is significantly less than just looking at the time shows. For full body, this along with trying to fit a 3+ hour block of time into a day often makes it worth it to consider the other two splits. Now, for push-pull-legs vs upper-lower splits, I've noticed that it often leaves me feeling like I had to pick between having a pull-focused day versus a push-focused when I was doing upper body. This eventually made me wonder if it would be better to revert back to Push-Pull-Legs. Currently, that is the experiment I am running now.
